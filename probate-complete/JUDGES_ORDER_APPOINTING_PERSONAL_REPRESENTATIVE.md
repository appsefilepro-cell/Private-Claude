☑ Taking oath of office (attached)

---

### ORDER 3: POWERS & DUTIES

The Personal Representative shall have the power and duty to:

**A. Asset Management:**
1. Take possession and control of all estate assets
2. Collect debts owed to the estate
3. Manage and preserve property
4. Invest funds prudently
5. Continue or operate decedent's business (if applicable)

**B. Financial Obligations:**
1. Pay estate debts and expenses
2. File tax returns (income, estate, property)
3. Pay taxes owed by estate
4. Defend or prosecute lawsuits

**C. Administration:**
1. Maintain accurate records and accounting
2. File inventory and appraisal within 4 months (Probate Code § 8800)
3. Publish notice to creditors
4. Review and pay/reject creditor claims
5. Prepare final accounting and petition for distribution

**D. Distribution:**
1. Distribute assets to heirs/beneficiaries (with notice)
2. Obtain receipts from distributees
3. Request discharge upon completion

---

### ORDER 4: INDEPENDENT ADMINISTRATION AUTHORITY

Pursuant to California Probate Code § 10400 et seq., the Personal Representative may take the following actions **WITHOUT PRIOR COURT APPROVAL**, but with notice to interested parties:

1. **Sale of Real Property:**
   - Notice of Proposed Action required (15 days)
   - Any heir may object (triggers court hearing)

2. **Sale of Personal Property:**
   - Notice of Proposed Action required (15 days)

3. **Borrowing Money:**
   - With estate assets as collateral (with notice)

4. **Leasing Property:**
   - Terms up to 10 years

5. **Payment of Claims:**
   - Allowed claims may be paid without court order

6. **Preliminary Distribution:**
   - With notice and consents

---

### ORDER 5: RESTRICTIONS

The Personal Representative may **NOT** do the following without court approval:

1. Sell real property at less than 90% of appraised value
2. Make gifts of estate property
3. Borrow money secured by estate assets exceeding $_________
4. Sell property to Personal Representative or interested party
5. Enter into contracts exceeding _________ years
6. Make final distribution

---

### ORDER 6: BLOCKED ACCOUNT

All cash and liquid assets shall be deposited in a **blocked account** at [Name of Bank] requiring court order for withdrawal exceeding $_________.

Account title: "Estate of Thurman Earl Robinson Sr., Thurman Robinson (Jr.), Personal Representative"

---

### ORDER 7: INVENTORY & APPRAISAL

The Personal Representative shall:

1. File **Inventory and Appraisal** (Probate Code Form DE-160) within **4 months** from date of issuance of letters.

2. Hire probate referee for appraisal of estate assets (except real property, which may be appraised by licensed real estate appraiser).

3. Serve copies on all interested parties.

---

### ORDER 8: ACCOUNTINGS

The Personal Representative shall file:

1. **First Accounting:** Within 1 year of appointment (if estate remains open)

2. **Annual Accountings:** Thereafter, if estate administration continues

3. **Final Accounting:** Upon completion of administration, with Petition for Final Distribution

---

### ORDER 9: NOTICE TO CREDITORS

The Personal Representative shall:

1. **Publish Notice to Creditors** in [Newspaper Name] once a week for 4 consecutive weeks.

2. **Mail Notice to Known Creditors** within 4 months of appointment.

3. **Deadline for creditor claims:**
   - 4 months from first publication, OR
   - 60 days from mailing of notice to known creditor
   - Whichever is later

---

### ORDER 10: COMPENSATION

The Personal Representative is entitled to:

1. **Statutory compensation** pursuant to California Probate Code § 10800:
   - 4% of first $100,000
   - 3% of next $100,000
   - 2% of next $800,000
   - 1% of next $9 million
   - 0.5% of next $15 million
   - Reasonable amount above $25 million

2. **Extraordinary fees** for unusual services (e.g., litigation, business operations)

3. **Reimbursement** for reasonable expenses incurred
