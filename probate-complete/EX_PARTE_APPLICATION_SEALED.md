## VII. MEMORANDUM OF POINTS AND AUTHORITIES

**A. Legal Basis:**

1. **California Probate Code § 8540** - Special Administrator may be appointed when:
   - Delay in granting letters would cause loss or injury to estate
   - Immediate appointment is necessary to preserve estate

2. **California Probate Code § 8545** - Special Administrator has powers specified by court, including:
   - Taking possession of estate property
   - Preserving assets
   - Preventing waste

3. **California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1203** - Ex parte applications permitted when:
   - Immediate harm would result from delay
   - Notice would defeat purpose of application

**B. Case Law:**

1. *Estate of Sapp* (1979) - Court may appoint special administrator to prevent waste or loss of assets

2. *Estate of Kraus* (2010) - Ex parte relief appropriate when estate assets are at immediate risk
