DRAFTING TIPS:

1. Be specific with facts - use dates, amounts, names
2. Quote policy language exactly
3. Cite relevant statutes by number
4. Quantify damages precisely
5. Set realistic but firm deadline
6. Show you've done research (case citations)
7. Preserve evidence formally
8. Make litigation consequences clear
9. Leave room for settlement
10. Professional tone throughout

AVOID:
- Emotional language
- Threats that can't be backed up
- Vague allegations
- Excessive length (keep under 5 pages)
- Revealing strategy details
- Making it too easy to ignore

The goal is to demonstrate strength while providing a clear path to resolution.
