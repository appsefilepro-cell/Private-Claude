## V. LEGAL BASIS - VIOLATION OF TEXAS PROPERTY CODE § 92.008

### TEXAS PROPERTY CODE § 92.008 - LANDLORD'S DUTY NOT TO INTERRUPT UTILITIES

**(a) A landlord may not interrupt or cause the interruption of utility service paid for directly to the utility company by a tenant unless the interruption results from bona fide repairs, construction, or an emergency.**

**(b) A landlord may not interrupt or cause the interruption of water, wastewater, gas, or electric service as a means to force the tenant to vacate the premises.**

**(c) A landlord who violates this section is liable to the tenant for:**
- **Actual damages;**
- **One month's rent plus $500;**
- **Attorney's fees; and**
- **Court costs.**

**(d) A tenant may:**
**(1) file a repair and remedy lawsuit;**
**(2) terminate the lease; or**
**(3) obtain injunctive relief.**
