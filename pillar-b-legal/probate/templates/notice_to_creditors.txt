CLAIMS INFORMATION

Persons who have claims against the estate must present them as follows:

CLAIM PRESENTATION DEADLINE: [DEADLINE DATE]

A claim is a demand for payment of a debt of the deceased or an entitlement to
payment of an estate debt. For information about how to present a claim and the
requirements for supporting documentation, see Probate Code Section 1250 et seq.

The personal representative may either pay the claim, pass on the claim, or
reject the claim. If the personal representative does not act on the claim
within 30 days after receiving the claim, the claim will be deemed allowed and
the personal representative will be liable for the full amount of the claim.

SPECIAL NOTICE TO FAMILY MEMBERS AND CREDITORS

The law allows 120 days from the date letters are first issued for creditors to
make claims against the estate. This notice period cannot be extended.

If you do not receive this notice and have a claim against the estate, you must
still file your claim by the deadline specified above or you will lose your right
to collect from the estate.

TO PRESERVE YOUR LEGAL RIGHTS, YOU MUST ACT PROMPTLY. If you are uncertain about
your legal rights, you should consult with an attorney.

For more information about probate, see the California Courts website at
www.courts.ca.gov/selfhelp-probate.htm
