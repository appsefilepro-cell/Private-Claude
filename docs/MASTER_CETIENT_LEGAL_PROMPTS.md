## THE "7-CYCLE FORGE" METHODOLOGY

Every document must pass through these 7 internal iterations before output:

### Cycle 1: SCAFFOLDING
- Establish document structure and hierarchical organization
- Create comprehensive table of contents
- Define all major sections and subsections
- Set up heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3, etc.)
- Establish preliminary page count targets
- Insert section placeholders and cross-references

### Cycle 2: CORE DRAFT
- Insert all factual allegations with precision
- Draft initial legal arguments with supporting authority
- Include all necessary parties and jurisdictional elements
- Establish timeline of events in chronological order
- Draft preliminary statement of facts
- Insert cause of action elements with citations

### Cycle 3: NARRATIVE ENHANCEMENT ("Words of Power")
- Transform facts into compelling narrative storytelling
- Employ persuasive language techniques
- Create emotional resonance while maintaining professionalism
- Develop thematic consistency throughout document
- Craft powerful introductory and concluding paragraphs
- Use parallel structure and rhetorical devices strategically

### Cycle 4: FORTIFICATION (Triple-Support Evidentiary Linking)
- Verify every factual assertion has at least three supporting sources
- Link all legal arguments to controlling authority
- Cross-reference exhibits and supporting documentation
- Ensure chain of evidence is unbroken
- Add backup citations for all primary authority
- Insert protective alternative arguments

### Cycle 5: REFINEMENT (Clarity and Punch)
- Eliminate redundancy and wordiness
- Strengthen weak arguments or remove them entirely
- Enhance transitions between sections
- Verify logical flow and argumentative progression
- Polish language for maximum impact
- Ensure every sentence serves a strategic purpose

### Cycle 6: FORMATTING (Bluebook Citation Check)
- Verify all citations comply with Bluebook 21st Edition
- Standardize case name formatting (italics, abbreviations)
- Check all statute citations for accuracy
- Verify all pinpoint citations to specific pages
- Ensure proper use of Id., supra, and infra
- Format all footnotes correctly

### Cycle 7: FINAL POLISH (The "Murder Board" Review)
- Conduct adversarial review from opposing counsel perspective
- Identify and address all weaknesses
- Verify no procedural defects exist
- Confirm all filing requirements are met
- Final proofreading for typographical errors
- Generate final table of contents and table of authorities

## MANDATORY FORMATTING STANDARDS

### Pleading Paper Requirements
- **Standard:** 28-line numbered paper for all court filings
- **Margins:** 1-inch on all sides (some jurisdictions require 1.5-inch left margin)
- **Font:** Times New Roman, 12-point for body text
- **Line Spacing:** Double-spaced body text, single-spaced footnotes
- **Page Numbering:** Bottom center, starting with page 2

### Caption and Header Format
```

                               )
     v.                        )  [DOCUMENT TITLE]
                               )

                               )

```

### IRAC Structure (Legal Memoranda)
**Issue:** State the legal question precisely and concisely
- Frame as a question
- Include key facts necessary to understand the issue
- Be specific to the case at hand

**Rule:** State the applicable legal rule(s)
- Cite controlling authority (constitutions, statutes, regulations, case law)
- Include exact language of relevant statutes or holdings
- Explain any elements or tests that apply

**Application:** Apply the rule to the facts
- Connect each element of the rule to specific facts
- Use analogical reasoning with precedent cases
- Address counter-arguments and distinguish adverse authority
- Explain why the rule supports your position

**Conclusion:** State the answer to the issue
- Provide definitive answer when possible
- Acknowledge uncertainty if genuinely exists
- Summarize the strongest supporting reasons

**Case Citations:**
- Supreme Court: *Brown v. Board of Educ.*, 347 U.S. 483, 495 (1954).

## DOCUMENT TYPES AND MINIMUM STANDARDS

### Complaints (Minimum 25 pages)
- Caption and parties identification
- Jurisdictional statement (subject matter and personal)
- Factual allegations (chronological narrative)
- All causes of action with element-by-element allegations
- Prayer for relief (specific and general damages)
- Jury demand (if applicable)
- Signature block and verification

### Motions (Minimum 15 pages)
- Notice of motion with hearing date
- Memorandum of points and authorities
- Statement of facts with evidentiary support
- Legal argument with case law analysis
- Conclusion with specific requested relief
- Supporting declarations under penalty of perjury

### Briefs (Minimum 30 pages)
- Table of contents
- Table of authorities (cases, statutes, other)
- Statement of jurisdiction
- Issues presented
- Statement of the case
- Statement of facts
- Summary of argument
- Argument (with IRAC structure for each point)
- Conclusion
- Signature and certificate of service

### Legal Memoranda (Minimum 20 pages)
- Question(s) presented
- Brief answer
- Statement of facts
- Discussion (IRAC format)
- Conclusion
- Recommendations (if advisory memo)

### Discovery Responses (Minimum 10 pages per set)
- General objections
- Specific objections to each request
- Substantive responses with full detail
- Privilege log (if applicable)
- Verification and signature
